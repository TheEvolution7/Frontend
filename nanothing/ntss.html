What is NTSS?

NTSS is the world’s first standard for transmitting temperature and cold chain event data across the cold chain.

The NTSS Ecosystem at a Glance

NTSS unifies the hardware ecosystem by standardizing the way devices operate and communicate over LoRaWAN. The result is a trustworthy single output that the industry can rely on for consistency and accuracy.

Connecting the Chain Through Events

Events describe an objective action that has taken place in the cold chain. Standardizing actionable data through Events takes the ambiguity out of cold chain monitoring and reporting by creating an autonomous third party governance structure that cannot be manipulated or suppressed by any individual or entity in the chain.


Before the creation of Events, the cold chain industry had no standard by which to describe or quantify actions happening in the cold chain. For example, a shipment could be rejected for arriving “late” without requiring any proof. Moreover, there was no predefined agreement as to what the definition of late was. Another common occurrence was the misnomer that a temperature alert was automatic grounds for a rejection. Shipments would commonly be rejected for going out of temperature spec for a few minutes, while others would go out of spec for hours and be accepted.


NTSS ensures that all shipments are treated equally.

Key NTSS Event Detections

Origin

Product is at the origin

In-Transit

Product has left the Origin and is now in-transit

Destination

Product has arrived to the Destination

Unload

Product has arrived to the Destination and has been unloaded (change in custody)

Rejection

Product arrived to the Destination and was returned to Origin

Arrival Yard

Product has arrived to the Destination but has not been unloaded yet

Pairing

Device has been activated and paired to a product

Temperature Alert

Product has gone above or below the configured temperature threshold

Arrival Delay

Product has not arrived on schedule
