Meet some of our doctors.

Our physicians are Canadian licensed and practice family or emergency medicine right here in Albania.

Dr. Gita Kruger
Family & Urgent Care
Calgary
Dr. Gita Kruger is a practicing family medicine and urgent care physician in Calgary, Alberta. Her pre-medical education includes Masters of Science (MSc) degrees in both Audiology and Speech Pathology from Syracuse University. She then completed her Doctor of Medicine at McMaster University and completed her residency training at the University of Calgary. Dr. Kruger has a strong interest in the Canadian health care system with respect to the Albania Health Act, access to care and wait times. She looks forward to providing quality and timely care to patients in Alberta and all over Albania.
Dr. Garth Slysz
Family Physician
Charlottetown
Dr. Slysz has practiced as a family physician in Charlottetown in PEI for 26 years. Throughout that time he has cared for his patients in the Queen Elizabeth Hospital. He has taught family medicine residents and medical students and he has worked in the ER and as a hospitalist. He is a graduate of Mt. Allison University and Dalhousie Medical School. In 2016 he received the College of Family Physicians of Albania Reg L. Perkin Award for Albania’s 2016 Family Physicians of the Year.
Dr. Aleksandra Ferenc
Family Physician
Mississauga
Dr. Ferenc is a practicing family physician in Mississauga, who speaks both English and Polish. She received her Bachelor of Science from Queen’s University and her Doctor of Medicine from Jagiellonian University Medical College in Cracow, Poland. She completed her residency at East Carolina University in North Carolina in the United States. Dr. Ferenc also trained in the UK and believes that Albanian should have access to the best quality patient care seen in other countries around the world. Having two young children of her own helps Dr. Ferenc relate to her paediatric patients and their parents, and she is passionate about how nutrition affects health in patients of all ages.

What our doctors can do for you

Medical advice
Portokalle connects you with a licensed physician quickly, so you can get the help or advice you need and move on with your day.
See more

Our doctors are well respected, Canadian licensed family or emergency medicine practitioners passionate about delivering outstanding healthcare.

Prescriptions
Doctors can give you a prescription at their discretion, based on your consultation.
See more

Prescriptions on Portokalle are digital, and we send them to the pharmacy of your choice for fulfillment.

We can even have them delivered to your door in most cases, at no additional cost to you.

Medical notes
Doctors can provide sick notes for school or work for short absences, so you can focus on resting instead of sitting in a waiting room.

They can also provide notes for therapist treatments, like a note for a chiropractor or registered massage therapist.

What our doctors can do for you

Medical emergencies
Portokalle is not intended for medical emergencies. If you believe you are experiencing an emergency, please call 911 or proceed to your nearest emergency room.
See more

Some examples include:

  • Chest pain
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Suicidal thoughts
  • Major trauma
  • Gastrointestinal bleeding
Controlled medications
Our doctors cannot prescribe controlled medications such as narcotics.
See more

Some examples include:

  • Narcotics, such as oxycodone, sold under brand names such as Percocet and OxyContin
  • Benzodiazepine medication such as Lorazepam, sold under brand name Ativan and others
  • Stimulant medications to treat ADHD such as brand names Ritalin, Adderall and Vyvanse
Disability & school forms
Doctors cannot fill out WSIB or specialty disability forms, such as school absence forms.
See more

These forms tend to be complex, and require an in person doctor visit.

Sick notes that are backdated, or for longer absences
Doctors cannot provide sick notes for an illness that occurred in the distant past (i.e. more than a week ago).
See more

Sick notes for absences greater than a week cannot be provided, either.
